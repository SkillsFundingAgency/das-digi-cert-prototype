Set up an apprenticeship: step by step

Check what you need to do to set up an apprenticeship.

  1. Step 1: Have an apprentice

    If you don’t have an existing apprentice, you can create an advert to recruit one.

    1. Recruit an apprentice

  2. and Add or confirm your training provider

    You need to find a training provider that offers the apprenticeship course that your apprentice will take.

    You will need to agree to the training costs and the apprentice plan directly with your training provider.

    Once you have a training provider, you need to give them permissions and link them to your account.

    1. Find a training provider
    2. Add training provider and set permissions

  3. Step 2: Apply for funding

    If do not pay the apprenticeship levy, there are three ways to fund an apprenticeship.

    Government funding

    The government will pay 95% towards the cost of training and assessing your apprentice, meaning you will need to pay 5%.

    You can reserve government funding for an apprenticeship for 6 months. If you do not use it in this time it will expire, and you will need to reserve funding again.

    Pledges set up by apprenticeship levy-paying businesses

    You can apply to receive funds that levy-paying businesses offer to smaller businesses.

    If your application is successful, this will cover 100% of your apprenticeship training and assessment costs (up to the funding band maximum).

    Direct transfer of funds

    You can also arrange for a levy-paying business that you know to transfer funds directly to you, but they must invite you to do this from their employer account.

    This will also cover 100% of your apprenticeship training and assessment costs (up to the funding band maximum).

    1. Government funding (reservations)
    2. Pledges set up by apprenticeship levy-paying businesses
    3. Direct transfer of funds

  4. Step 3: Add apprentice details

    Add the apprentice details or request that your training provider does this to finish setting up the apprenticeship.

    1. Add apprentice(s)
